## 日文

敬語の使い分け（けいごのつかいわけ）

**羅馬拼音**：keigo no tsukaiwake

**詞性**：名詞

## 日文解釋

「敬語の使い分け」とは、異なる人間関係や場面に応じて、適切な敬語を選択し、使い分けることを指します。日本語の敬語は複雑で、主に三つのカテゴリーに分類されます：①尊敬語[^sonkeigo]（相手や第三者を敬う表現）②謙譲語[^kenjougo]（自分や自分の側の人を低める表現）③丁寧語[^teineigo]（文末を丁寧にする表現）。ビジネス環境[^business]では、上司、同僚、顧客など異なる立場の人との関係に基づいて、適切な敬語レベルを選択することが極めて重要です。敬語の使い分けができないと、相手に失礼な印象を与え、信頼関係を損なう可能性があります。敬語は日本文化における人間関係の距離感と尊重の表現方法の核となる要素です。

## 英文解釋

"Keigo no tsukaiwake" refers to the appropriate selection and use of different levels of honorific language (keigo)[^keigo] based on interpersonal relationships and situational contexts. Japanese honorific language is complex and generally categorized into three main types: ① Sonkeigo[^sonkeigo] (respectful language used to honor others) ② Kenjougo[^kenjougo] (humble language used to lower oneself) ③ Teineigo[^teineigo] (polite language primarily affecting the sentence ending). In business environments[^business], it is crucial to select the appropriate level of honorific language based on your relationship with different people such as superiors, colleagues, and customers. Failure to properly distinguish between keigo levels can give an unfavorable impression and damage trust relationships. The proper use of keigo is a fundamental element of Japanese culture that reflects interpersonal distance and respect.

## 中文解釋

「敬語的區分使用」指的是根據不同的人際關係和場景，選擇並使用適當的敬語水準。日語敬語複雜，主要分為三類：①敬語[^sonkeigo]（用來尊敬對方或第三者的表達方式）②謙讓語[^kenjougo]（用來貶低自己或自己一方的表達方式）③敬語[^teineigo]（主要影響句子結尾的禮貌表達）。在商業環境[^business]中，根據與上司、同事和客戶等不同身份人士的關係，選擇適當的敬語水準極為重要。未能正確區分敬語水準會給對方留下無禮的印象，並損害信任關係。敬語是日本文化中反映人際距離和尊重的核心要素。

## 註解

[^keigo]: **敬語（けいご）** - Honorific language; a linguistic system in Japanese used to show respect and maintain appropriate social distance. One of the most distinctive features of the Japanese language

[^sonkeigo]: **尊敬語（そんけいご）** - Respectful language used when talking about or to someone of higher status or when referring to someone else's actions or attributes. Example: 先生がおっしゃる（The teacher says）instead of 先生が言う

[^kenjougo]: **謙讓語（けんじょうご）** - Humble language used to lower oneself or one's actions, often used when referring to one's own or one's company's actions in formal/business contexts. Example: 申し上げます（I humbly state）instead of 言います

[^teineigo]: **丁寧語（ていねいご）** - Polite language primarily affecting the sentence ending with ～です and ～ます forms, the most basic level of honorific expression

[^business]: **ビジネス環境（びじねすかんきょう）** - Business environment; formal workplace contexts where keigo distinctions are especially important for maintaining professional relationships and corporate culture
